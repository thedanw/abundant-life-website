**Content:**

Abundant Life Centre is part of The Australian Christian Churches [ACC](formerly called Assemblies of God in Australia). ACC was formed out of a conference of the Assemblies of God Queensland and the Pentecostal Church of Australia in Sydney, Easter 1937.

In 1983, Ps Neville & Jenney Wedrat, who pastored Tableland Christian Faith Centre in Atherton, burned with a vision to plant a Assemblies of God Church in Mareeba. Meetings commenced in August 1983 on Sundays at 4pm at the Mareeba State High school led by Ps Neville, Ps Jenney and their team.

Many people passionately served, sowed, & prayed into this vision until in 1985, Ps Barry & Linda Wedrat were released from Calvary Church in Townsville to take responsibility of the work in Mareeba.

In May, 1985, Mareeba AOG began to have meetings on Sunday mornings at the Mareeba State Primary School. Ps Barry and Linda served faithfully until the call of God led them to accept the role of Senior Pastor at Townsville City church in mid 1990.

Ps Dennis & Jane Lumley accepted the invitation to Mareeba & arrived in April 1991. In 1992, the name of the church was changed to "Abundant Life Centre" and became a registered church on the 19th August 1992. Dennis & Jane faithfully served the community until the call led them to accept an assistant pastors role at Cairns Christian Centre (Freshwater) in January 1997.

On the 6th of January 1997, Ps Darryl & Patricia Roos accepted the Senior Pastors role & continue to lead the house today.

**Title:** WHAT WE BELIEVE - ALC Mareeba

**Content:**

#### Statement of Faith
- The Bible is the inspired and only infallible and authoritative Word of God. 2 Tim 3:16; 2 Pet 1:19-21
- There is one God, eternally existent in three Persons; God the Father, God the Son and God the Holy Spirit. Mat 28:19; 2 Cor 13:14
- In the Deity of our Lord Jesus Christ, His virgin birth, his sinless life, miracles, his vicarious and atoning death, his bodily resurrection, his ascension to the right hand of the Father, his personal future return to this earth in power and in glory to rule for a thousand years.
- In the blessed hope: The rapture of the church at Christ's coming 1 Thess 4:17. The resurrection of the saved and lost, the one to everlasting life and the other to everlasting damnation. Dan 12:2; John 11:25-26
- The only way to be cleansed from sin is through repentance and faith in the precious blood of Christ. Eph1:7
- Regeneration by the Holy Spirit is absolutely essential for personal salvation. John 1:3; 3:3
- The baptism of the Holy Spirit is given to believers who ask. Acts 2:4; Luke 11:13
- In the present day reality of the supernatural operations of the gifts of the Holy Spirit. 1 Cor 12:8-10
- The redemptive work of Christ on the cross provides Healing for Body in answer to believing prayer. Isaiah 53:4-5; Mat 8:17
- In the observance of Christian Baptism by immersion and the Lord's Supper. Matt 28:19; Acts 2:38

#### Gathering Together as Christ's Body
One of the most fulfilling experiences in the life of a believer is to gather together to worship God with other Christians. The Bible uses expressions such as ''the Family of God'' and the ''Body of Christ'' to depict the beautiful relationship of love and commitment that Christians have for each other under the Lordship of Jesus, who is the head of the Church. As members of the Family of God, it is imperative that we gather together to fulfill our family role. The writer of the book of Hebrews stresses that we should not forsake our gathering together, Heb 10:25. We believe that nothing can substitute for the Body gathering together for the purpose of worship, ministry and fellowship. For it is within this context, that spiritual gifts operate, corporate worship takes place, people are ministered to, and the Body of Christ is taught to be effective in the task of reaching the world. Hence, the local church is crucial to achievement of the purposes of God.

#### Three-fold Purpose of Gathering
The purpose of our gathering is three-fold. Firstly, to minister to God in worship, praise and adoration. Secondly, to minister that we might be equipped to minister to the world. Thirdly, to fellowship with one another.

Our pattern of worship that we follow is established for us in the bible. ''But the hour is coming, and now is, when the true worshipers shall worship the Father in spirit and in truth; for such people, the Father seeks to be His worshipers. God is spirit and those who worship Him must worship in spirit and in truth" John 4:23-24.

We believe that in order to worship God in spirit, we must worship Him with our entire being. So, we clap our hands, lift our voice and sing songs of praise unto God. Our worship forms are built on the Bible pattern.

#### Clapping and Lifting Hands - Audible Praise
Old Testament writer, David, said, " clap your hands all ye people, shout to God with a voice of triumph." Ps 47:1. The Psalms also speak of lifting up Holy hands in an attitude of surrender and worship to God. Ps 63:4; 1 Tim 2:8. The Apostle Paul, a New Testament writer said "Let us offer a sacrifice of praise to God continually that is the fruit of our lips giving thanks to his name." Heb 13:15. Audible praise is part of our natural response to the love and grace of God we have experienced in our lives.

#### Singing and Musical Instruments
Our songs of Praise to God include those sung in our native language and those we sing in heavenly language. Musical instruments are used because the Psalmist says, ''praise Him with the sound of the trumpet, praise Him with the psaltery and harp. praise Him with the timbrel and dance. Praise Him with stringed instruments, praise with the loud cymbals...'' Ps 150:3-5.

#### Tithes and offerings
Another aspect of our worship to God is when we give our tithes and offering to him. We gladly bring our offerings and give thanks for His goodness and blessings in our lives. As our gifts are collected we join together in a vocal expression of praise and worship to God believing that God is our source and thanking him for His provision in our lives. In prayer, we visualize our tithes and offering being used specifically for the extension of God's kingdom. 2 Cor 9; Mal 3:10; Luke 6:38; Duet 16:17.

Part of the function of the Body of Christ is to minister to one another and edify one another so that we might be more effective in reaching out and helping those who are in need. For this reason, Jesus has given every individual Christian special ministry gifts which are operated for the common good of the church.

#### Prophecy
One of the gifts of edification and ministry that God has given us is the gift of prophecy. Prophecy is a message from God for the Family of God spoken in the native tongue. The bible says, " but one who prophecies speak to men for edification, exhortation and consolation." 1 Cor 14:3

#### Messages in Tongues with Interpretation
A message in tongues with the accompanying interpretation is another spiritual gift often employed in our service. Paul wrote, "...to each one is given the manifestation (ministry gift) of the spirit of common good... to another various kinds of tongues, and to another the interpretation of tongues." 1 Cor 12:7-10. A message in tongues is simply God speaking to His church through a human vessel. In order for the church to understand this message, another person interprets the message. Hence the people are strengthened and edified.

#### Preaching and Teaching Gods Word
The bible says, "you shall know the truth and the truth shall set you free." John 8:32. Preaching and teaching God's word has a two-fold purpose. Firstly, the scriptures are taught and explained in such a way that it will cause spiritual growth in the people of God. Secondly, the truth of God's word is presented in a way that will encourage people to find Jesus as their saviour. People are set free and launched in to a richer life with Christ. By sharing in God's word, Christians are taught to be more effective in their ministry to the world. Eph 4.

#### Other Gifts
There are many other forms of ministry we perform as members of the Body of Christ. Some are operated publically in the context of our services, and some others behind the scene, all with the purpose of building up the body of Christ and reaching the lost with the gospel.

As the family of God, we especially enjoy the company and fellowship of other Christians in our services and especially before and after, as we show love for one another as Christ showed his love for us. 1 John 4: 7; 1 Pet 3:8

#### Quietness in Worship
Along with audible and joyful praise and worship to God, the psalmist encourages us to have time in silent prayer and meditation. "Be still and know that I am God..." Ps 46:10. The Apostle Paul wrote, "meditate on these things... that thy profiting might appear to all." 1 Tim 4:15. quietness in worship provides the opportunity for meditation, personal spiritual evaluation with God, thanksgiving and spiritual renewal.

#### Communion
A very essential part of our worship as the Body of Christ is in the sharing of communion. The communion bread represents Jesus' broken body and the wine represents His shed blood. In partaking of these representative elements, Christians reflect on the supreme sacrifice Jesus Christ made when he died on the cross for our sin. Matt 26:26-29. The only qualification for enjoying communion with us is that you are a believer. As a believer please join us in this celebration as we remember our Lord Jesus Christ.

#### Your Place in the Body
Remember in the Body of Christ, everyone is eventually important. We each have a special function to fulfill. Each of us must find his/her place of ministry and service in the Body of Christ and then grow up to the ministry, which is conformity to the person of Jesus Christ.

#### Prayer for Healing
God's purpose is for His people to be whole in every area of their lives, spiritually, mentally, socially and physically. When God's people pray, real spiritual power is generated. God's presence is generated and miracles take place. James 5:14-15

#### Prayer Needs
The bible says, "...if two of you shall agree on earth about anything that they may ask, it shall be done for them by my Father who is in heaven" Mat 18:19. Therefore, as the family of God, we bring prayer requests for the needs of every kind to the Body, for the purpose of agreeing together that the power of God will be manifested to meet those needs. We believe the prayer of God's people as one voice is the most powerful force in the world. join us in this effective, practical ministry to one another.

### 6. MISSIONS - http://www.abundantlifemareeba.org.au/missions

**Title:** Slideshow - ALC Mareeba

**Alphacrucis College** is a tertiary Christian liberal arts college. In addition to being the largest self-accrediting Christian liberal arts college in Australia, it is the official training college of Australian Christian Churches, the Assemblies of God in Australia. The College has campuses in every state capital city in Australia, campuses in Auckland and in Finland, and registered sites on offer in other places. Its main campus in Parramatta, New South Wales, Australia.
www.ac.edu.au

**Living Waters Village** is a 280-hectare village in the jungle of Kalimantan, Borneo that cares for 570 orphans and neglected children. Once the project is completed, it is estimated that the village will have accommodation for 1000 children and schooling for 2000 other children from surrounding villages.
www.livingwatersvillage.com

**Gideons International** is an evangelical Christian association founded in 1899 in Wisconsin. The Gideons' primary activity is distributing copies of the Bible free of charge. This Bible distribution is a global enterprise taking place in some 200 countries, territories and possessions.
www.gideons.org.au

**IMLA** is a cross-denominational apostolic network partnering with Aboriginal Christians in disciple making ministry. Based in Mareeba, Queensland, Australia, Our central strategy could be described biblically as "Equipping the Saints." (Eph 4:12) Through God's Word, ministry training, mentoring, networking and resourcing, Aboriginal Christians are empowered to fulfill their God-given visions. Emphasis is given to building relationships and communicating effectively to meet the needs of geographically and culturally isolated peoples.
www.iml.org.au

**School chaplains** are in the prevention and rescue business. They're helping students find a better way to deal with issues ranging from family breakdown and loneliness to drug abuse, stress and anxiety. They provide a listening ear and a caring presence for kids in crisis… and those who just need a friend. All have a passion to improve the lives of young people
www.suqld.org.au

**ApostleNet International** exists to equip and empower church and marketplace leaders to build the Kingdom of God in their sphere of influence.
